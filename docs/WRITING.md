# Writing Guidelines

This document turns the Mitty & Co voice into practical rules for product copy, marketing copy, and UI states.

## Core Writing Rules

1. **Highlight the Hand:** Always remind the user that hands made this. Use words like "sculpted," "painted by hand," and "molded."
2. **Embrace Earth:** Remind the user of the material. "Earthen clay," "sun-dried," "baked," "kiln."
3. **Bridge the Gap:** Highlight that the jewellery is for *both* traditional and modern wear. "Sarees, dresses, and every mood in between."
4. **Be Unapologetic about Uniqueness:** If a piece is sold out or unrepeatable, frame it as a testament to the art, not a stock issue. 

## Approved Brand Lines

Use these where appropriate across the site:
- Traditional clay. Modern expression.
- Rooted in Indian craft, designed for today.
- No two handmade pieces are exactly the same.
- Made for sarees, dresses, and every mood in between.
- Fresh from the kiln.
- Soulful clay, sculpted by hand.

## Category Copy Placeholders

Use these narratives when introducing categories:
- **Terracotta Sets:** "Complete statements of wearable art. Our necklace sets bring traditional motifs to life, perfect for when you want to carry a piece of heritage with you."
- **Earrings:** "From traditional jhumkas to modern textured drops. Lightweight, face-framing art pieces for every occasion."
- **Gen-Z Sets:** "Playful, abstract, and bold. Earthen clay reimagined for the modern wardrobe."
- **Abstract Sets:** "Fluid shapes and unconventional forms. For those who wear art as their signature."
- **Hansuli:** "A homage to the timeless choker. Solid, striking, and elegantly curved to rest perfectly on the collarbone."
- **3D-Art Jewellery:** "Intricate sculpting that rises from the clay. Every petal and bead shaped with meticulous attention to detail."
- **Painted Sets:** "Canvas on clay. Delicate brushstrokes and rich colors applied by hand to traditional Indian motifs."

## Avoid

- Corporate e-commerce jargon (e.g., "Add to basket to maximize savings").
- Diminishing the craft (e.g., "cheap clay jewelry").
- Implying factory precision (e.g., "identical perfection").
