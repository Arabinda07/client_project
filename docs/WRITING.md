# Writing Guidelines

This document turns the **goonjaa** voice into practical rules for product copy, marketing copy, and UI states.

## Core Writing Rules

1. **Highlight the hand:** Remind the reader that hands made this. Use words like "sculpted," "painted by hand," and "molded."
2. **Return to earth:** Keep the material present. Use "earthen clay," "sun-dried," "baked," and "kiln."
3. **Bridge wardrobes:** Show that the jewellery belongs with sarees, dresses, and everyday wear.
4. **Respect uniqueness:** If a piece is sold out or unrepeatable, frame that as part of the work, not a stock problem.

## Approved Brand Lines

Use these where appropriate across the site:
- Traditional clay. Modern expression.
- Rooted in Indian craft, designed for today.
- No two handmade pieces are exactly the same.
- Made for sarees, dresses, and every mood in between.
- Fresh from the kiln.
- Soulful clay, sculpted by hand.

## Category Copy Placeholders

Use these narratives when introducing categories:
- **Terracotta Sets:** "Complete statements of wearable art. Necklace sets bring traditional motifs into modern wardrobes."
- **Earrings:** "From traditional jhumkas to modern textured drops. Lightweight, face-framing pieces for every occasion."
- **Gen-Z Sets:** "Playful, abstract, and bold. Earthen clay reimagined for the modern wardrobe."
- **Abstract Sets:** "Fluid shapes and unusual forms for people who wear art as their signature."
- **Hansuli:** "A homage to the timeless choker, solid and curved to rest on the collarbone."
- **3D-Art Jewellery:** "Sculpted details that rise from the clay. Every petal and bead is shaped by hand."
- **Painted Sets:** "Canvas on clay. Delicate brushstrokes and rich colors applied by hand."

## Avoid

- Corporate e-commerce jargon, such as "maximize savings."
- Diminishing the craft, such as "cheap clay jewellery."
- Factory precision language, such as "identical perfection."
