# Tone

Tone is how the voice adapts to context. **goonjaa** should stay recognizable across the shop, the artist story, checkout, and support.

## Tone Principles

1. Match the patience of the craft.
2. Speak as an artist, not a corporation.
3. Treat handmade differences as part of the work.
4. Welcome traditional and modern wearers.

## Tone Map By User Moment

| User Moment | Tone | Product Role | Avoid |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Landing/Home** | Warm, clear, inviting | Introduce the art and the artist | Aggressive sales copy |
| **Shop/Category** | Descriptive, curated | Guide the visual experience | Generic retail jargon |
| **About Page** | Personal, honest | Connect the wearer to the creator | Resume-style lists |
| **Bulk Orders** | Attentive, practical | Build trust around timing and repeat pieces | Factory language |
| **Cart/Checkout** | Clear, reassuring | Help the user finish without friction | Over-explaining |
| **Empty States** | Gentle, guiding | Send the user back to the shop | Dead ends |

## Tone By Surface

### Landing Page

Tone: clear, elegant, warm.

Use: hand-molded, earth, wearable art, tradition, modern.

Avoid: "buy now", "huge discounts", "factory direct".

Example: "Soulful clay, sculpted by hand."

### About Page

Tone: intimate and grounded.

Good: "goonjaa began with a love for drawing, music, and the grounding feel of clay."

Bad: "We are a leading provider of terracotta jewellery."

### Bulk Orders

Tone: practical and careful.

Good: "Repeat an existing design for your gathering."

Bad: "Submit a form and we will quote a price."

### Shop / Categories

Tone: curated and appreciative.

Good: "Fresh from the kiln," "Curated selections."

Bad: "Browse inventory."
