# Tone

Tone is how the voice adapts to context. Mitty & Co should remain recognizable across every surface, whether exploring the shop, reading the artist's story, or seeking support for a custom order.

## Tone Principles

1. Match the beauty and patience of the craft.
2. Speak as an artist, not a corporation.
3. Embrace the imperfections of handmade art.
4. Welcome all types of wearers (traditional and modern).

## Tone Map By User Moment

| User Moment | Tone | Product Role | Avoid |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Landing/Home** | Warm, inviting, elegant | Introduce the art and the artist | Salesy, aggressive |
| **Shop/Category** | Descriptive, curated | Guide the visual experience | Generic retail jargon |
| **About Page** | Personal, soulful, honest | Connect the wearer to the creator | Resume-like lists |
| **Custom Orders** | Collaborative, attentive | Build trust for bespoke work | Automated, cold |
| **Cart/Checkout** | Clear, reassuring | Ensure a smooth transaction | Over-complicated steps |
| **Empty States** | Gentle, guiding | Help the user discover | Dead ends |

## Tone By Surface

### Landing Page
Tone: clear, elegant, warm.
Use: hand-molded, earth, wearable art, tradition, modern.
Avoid: "buy now", "huge discounts", "factory direct".
Example: "Soulful clay, sculpted by hand."

### About Page
Tone: intimate, narrative-driven.
Good: "Mitty & Co started as a passion project, born from a love for traditional aesthetics and the grounding feel of clay."
Bad: "We are a leading provider of terracotta jewellery."

### Custom Orders
Tone: Collaborative and inspiring.
Good: "Commission a one-of-a-kind masterpiece tailored to your vision."
Bad: "Submit a form and we will quote a price."

### Shop / Categories
Tone: Curated and appreciative.
Good: "Fresh from the kiln," "Curated selections."
Bad: "Browse inventory."
